## TEAM ROLES (Every Pokemon on a team fills at least one role)

### Offensive Roles
- **Physical Sweeper**: Uses setup moves (Swords Dance, Dragon Dance) to sweep. High Atk/Spe. Examples: Garchomp, Blaziken, Roaring Moon, Great Tusk
- **Special Sweeper**: Uses setup (Nasty Plot, Calm Mind, Quiver Dance) to sweep. High SpA/Spe. Examples: Volcarona, Gholdengo, Iron Valiant, Walking Wake
- **Mixed Sweeper**: Can hit from both sides to break through specialized walls. Examples: Iron Valiant, Dragapult, Salamence
- **Wallbreaker**: Breaks through defensive walls with raw power. Choiced items, high BP moves. Examples: Chi-Yu, Raging Bolt, Ursaluna, Hoopa-Unbound
- **Revenge Killer**: Fast Pokemon with priority or Choice Scarf to KO weakened threats. Examples: Scizor (Bullet Punch), Kingambit (Sucker Punch), Dragapult (fast + Scarf)
- **Cleaner**: Fast Pokemon saved for late-game when threats are weakened. Examples: Choice Scarf Gholdengo, Booster Energy Iron Valiant

### Defensive Roles
- **Physical Wall**: High Def + HP. Absorbs physical hits. Examples: Dondozo, Corviknight, Skarmory, Toxapex, Great Tusk
- **Special Wall**: High SpD + HP. Absorbs special hits. Examples: Blissey, Clodsire, Ting-Lu, Slowking
- **Mixed Wall**: High Def AND SpD. Examples: Ferrothorn, Toxapex, Garganacl, Corviknight
- **Pivot**: Switches in and out using U-turn/Volt Switch/Flip Turn/Teleport. Gains momentum. Examples: Landorus-Therian, Rotom-Wash, Corviknight, Dragapult, Meowscarada

### Utility Roles
- **Hazard Setter**: Sets entry hazards (Stealth Rock, Spikes, Toxic Spikes, Sticky Web). Examples: Hisuian Samurott, Glimmora, Ting-Lu, Ribombee (Web)
- **Hazard Remover**: Rapid Spin / Defog / Mortal Spin to clear hazards. Examples: Great Tusk (Spin), Iron Treads (Spin), Corviknight (Defog)
- **Status Spreader**: Inflicts status (Thunder Wave, Will-O-Wisp, Toxic, Spore). Examples: Rotom-Wash (Will-O-Wisp), Amoonguss (Spore), Dragapult (Thunder Wave)
- **Cleric**: Heals team's status with Heal Bell/Aromatherapy, or Wish passing. Examples: Blissey, Chansey, Sylveon, Alomomola (Wish)
- **Screens Setter**: Sets up Reflect/Light Screen for Hyper Offense. Examples: Alolan Ninetales (Aurora Veil), Grimmsnarl (Prankster dual screens)
- **Weather Setter**: Sets weather for weather teams. Examples: Pelipper (Drizzle), Torkoal (Drought), Tyranitar/Hippowdon (Sand Stream), Alolan Ninetales (Snow Warning)
- **Terrain Setter**: Sets terrain with ability or move. Examples: Tapu Koko (Electric Surge), Rillaboom (Grassy Surge), Indeedee (Psychic Surge), Tapu Fini (Misty Surge)

## TEAM ARCHETYPES

### 1. Hyper Offense (HO)
- Full offensive pressure, 5-6 sweepers/wallbreakers
- Usually has a Screens setter (Grimmsnarl/Alolan Ninetales) or suicide lead (Glimmora)
- No defensive switching — sacrifice attackers to bring in the next
- Speed control via priority moves, Choice Scarf, or Sticky Web
- Example: Grimmsnarl + Roaring Moon + Iron Valiant + Dragonite + Kingambit + Volcarona

### 2. Bulky Offense
- 3-4 offensive Pokemon + 2-3 bulky pivots
- Pivots bring in attackers safely
- Less frail than HO, can take some hits
- Most common playstyle in OU
- Example: Landorus-T + Rotom-Wash + Dragapult + Gholdengo + Great Tusk + Kingambit

### 3. Balance
- Equal mix: 2 walls, 2 pivots, 2 attackers (approximately)
- Aims to have a defensive answer to everything
- Focuses on hazard control and gradual damage
- Long-term planning, switching, predicting
- Example: Toxapex + Ferrothorn + Landorus-T + Tornadus-T + Garchomp + Dragapult

### 4. Semi-Stall
- 4 defensive Pokemon + 2 win conditions
- Wears down opponent with hazards + status
- Wincons clean up after team is weakened
- Example: Blissey + Toxapex + Skarmory + Clefable + setup sweeper + cleaner

### 5. Stall
- Full defense. 6 walls that cover all threats
- Wins by PP stalling and chip damage
- Relies on hazards, status, and passive recovery
- Example: Blissey + Dondozo + Corviknight + Clodsire + Garganacl + Toxapex

## TYPE SYNERGY — Building Defensive Cores

### Fire-Water-Grass Core (Classic)
The foundation of balanced teams. Each type covers the others' weaknesses:
- Fire covers Grass's Ice weakness, Water covers Fire's Ground/Rock weaknesses, Grass covers Water's Electric/Grass weaknesses
- Heatran + Gastrodon + Amoonguss
- Volcarona + Rotom-Wash + Rillaboom

### Dragon-Steel-Fairy Core (Modern)
The Gen VI+ standard defensive backbone:
- Dragon resists Fire/Water/Electric, Steel resists Dragon/Fairy/Ice, Fairy resists Fighting/Dark and is immune to Dragon
- Garchomp + Ferrothorn + Clefable
- Dragapult + Gholdengo + Iron Valiant
- Roaring Moon + Corviknight + Enamorus

### Steel-Fairy Core
- Steel covers Fairy's Steel/Poison weaknesses
- Fairy covers Steel's Fighting weakness
- Ferrothorn + Clefable / Corviknight + Sylveon

### Flying-Steel Core (SkarmBliss / Skarmory + Blissey variant)
- Flying immune to Ground, resists Fighting
- Steel resists Rock, Ice, Electric
- Corviknight + Gholdengo / Skarmory + Heatran

### Psychic-Dark-Fighting
Offensive core. Each type beats the others' checks:
- Fighting beats Dark/Steel (Dark resists), Dark beats Psychic/Ghost, Psychic beats Fighting/Poison
- Iron Valiant + Gholdengo + Kingambit

## OFFENSIVE SYNERGY

### Stacking Same-Type Threats
- Run multiple Pokemon of same type to overwhelm shared checks
- "Bird spam": Multiple strong Flying types to wear down Steel checks
- "Sand + Rock spam": Tyranitar + Excadrill + Garganacl
- "Dragon spam": Multiple Dragons to wear down Fairy/Steel checks

### Lure + Sweeper
- One mon baits in and weakens/kills the other's check
- Magnezone traps and kills Steel types so Dragon sweeper can clean
- Pursuit trappers remove Ghosts so Psychic sweeper can clean

### Dual Screens + Setup Sweeper
- Screens setter (Grimmsnarl, Alolan Ninetales) + setup sweeper that's frail
- Behind screens: Glass cannons become surprisingly bulky

### Weather Abuse
- Rain: Pelipper + Swift Swim sweepers (Barraskewda, Floatzel, Kingdra)
- Sun: Torkoal + Chlorophyll sweepers (Venusaur, Scovillain) + Protosynthesis mons (Roaring Moon, Flutter Mane)
- Sand: Tyranitar/Hippowdon + Sand Rush Excadrill + Rock/Ground/Steel types
- Snow: Alolan Ninetales + Slush Rush (Cetitan) + Ice types (defense boost in snow)

## TEAM BUILDING — Step by Step

### Step 1: Pick Your Pokemon (Core)
Start with the Pokemon you want to build around. Identify:
- What is it good at? (sweeping, wallbreaking, walling)
- What stops it? (counters, checks)
- What archetype does it fit? (HO, balance, weather, etc.)
- Does it need support? (hazard removal, screens, pivot support)

### Step 2: Add Defensive Synergy
- Can your team switch into common threats?
- Add Pokemon that cover the core's weaknesses
- Include at least one Ground immunity/resist, one Steel type, one Fairy type

### Step 3: Add Hazard Control
- Stealth Rock user (mandatory for any serious team)
- Hazard removal (Rapid Spin > Defog usually)
- Maybe: Spikes, Toxic Spikes, Sticky Web depending on archetype

### Step 4: Add Speed Control
- At least one fast attacker (naturally fast or Scarf)
- Priority moves for revenge killing
- Ways to handle opposing setup sweepers

### Step 5: Add Win Conditions
- Something that can end the game after team is prepared
- Setup sweeper, Choice item cleaner, or bulky wincon (Calm Mind Clefable, Curse Dondozo)

### Step 6: Check Meta Threats
- Can you handle top meta threats?
- Do you have an answer for: opposing setup, stall, weather, Trick Room?

## SPEED TIERS (important benchmarks at Lv50 and Lv100)

### Common Speed Benchmarks
- 200+ Speed: Ultra fast (Dragapult, Zacian, Pheromosa). Highest natural speed.
- 150-199: Very fast (Mega Lopunny, Mega Manectric). Outspeeds most meta.
- 130-149: Fast (Iron Bundle, Flutter Mane, Mega Gengar, Tapu Koko)
- 110-129: Above average (Latios, Mega Diancie, Gengar, Espeon)
- 100-109: Average baseline (Charizard, Salamence, Jirachi, Volcarona)
- 90-99: Slightly below average (Lucario, Roserade, Mega Scizor)
- 80-89: Slow (Venusaur, Dragonite, non-Mega Gyarados)
- 60-79: Slower (Tyranitar, Scizor, Heatran, Skarmory)
- 40-59: Very slow (Azumarill, Clefable, Corviknight, bulky pivots)
- <40: Ultra slow / Trick Room (Ferrothorn, Slowbro, Snorlax, Ursaluna)

### Speed Items/Abilities Benchmarks
- Choice Scarf: 1.5x Speed. Slow mon like Heatran (77 base) outspeeds base 110+.
- Swift Swim (Rain): 2x Speed. Barraskewda (136 base) becomes nearly impossible to outspeed.
- Chlorophyll (Sun): 2x Speed. Venusaur (80 base) becomes very fast.
- Sand Rush: 2x Speed in Sand. Excadrill (88 base) becomes excellent.
- Slush Rush: 2x Speed in Snow. Cetitan (73 base) becomes relevant.
- Unburden: 2x Speed when item consumed. Hawlucha activates + outspeeds the meta.
- Booster Energy: +1 Speed (1.5x). Iron Valiant, Iron Bundle become terrifying.
- Dragon Dance / Quiver Dance: +1 Speed per use. Garchomp after 1 DD outspeeds most.
- Sticky Web: -1 Speed for grounded opponents. Enables slower wallbreakers.

## COMPETITIVE ITEMS

### Damage Boosters
- Choice Band: 1.5x Atk, locked into one move. For physical wallbreakers.
- Choice Specs: 1.5x SpA, locked into one move. For special wallbreakers.
- Life Orb: 1.3x all damage but -10% HP per hit. For mixed attackers and sweepers.
- Expert Belt: 1.2x super-effective moves. Good on coverage-heavy attackers.
- Booster Energy: +1 to highest stat (consumed on switch-in). Paradox Pokemon meta.

### Speed Items
- Choice Scarf: 1.5x Speed, locked into one move. For revenge killers.
- Booster Energy: Can also boost Speed.

### Survivability
- Leftovers: 1/16 HP per turn. For defensive mons and bulky setup.
- Heavy-Duty Boots: Ignore entry hazards. Meta-defining item.
- Assault Vest: 1.5x SpD, no status moves. For bulky pivots.
- Rocky Helmet: Contact moves damage attacker. For physical walls.
- Focus Sash: Survive at 1 HP from full. For frail leads and sweepers.
- Air Balloon: Ground immunity until hit. For setup opportunities.

### Setup Items
- Light Clay: Extends screens to 8 turns. For screen setters.
- Heat/Damp/Icy/Smooth Rock: Extends weather. For weather teams.
- Terrain Extender: Extends terrain. For terrain teams.
- Mental Herb: Cures Taunt. For setup sweepers vulnerable to Taunt.
- White Herb: Cures stat drops. Good with Shell Smash or Superpower.
- Power Herb: Skip charge turn. For Solar Beam, Meteor Beam, Geomancy.

### Utility
- Eject Button: Switch on hit. Creates free switches.
- Red Card: Force out attacker on first hit. Anti-setup.
- Shed Shell: Escape Shadow Tag/Arena Trap. For defensive mons.

## POPULAR META POKEMON & ROLES

### Common Offensive Threats (Gen IX OU-style)
- **Dragapult**: Fast pivot, Choice Specs nuke, Will-O-Wisp spreader
- **Gholdengo**: Hazard blocker (Good as Gold), Choice Scarf cleaner, Nasty Plot breaker
- **Great Tusk**: Physical wallbreaker, Rapid Spin user, Stealth Rock setter
- **Kingambit**: Late-game sweeper (Supreme Overlord), Sucker Punch revenge killer
- **Iron Valiant**: Mixed sweeper, Booster Energy cleaner, Choice Specs wallbreaker
- **Roaring Moon**: Dragon Dance sweeper, Choice Band wallbreaker, Acrobatics + Booster Energy
- **Volcarona**: Quiver Dance sweeper, Flame Body pivot, devastating once boosted
- **Chi-Yu**: Special nuke with Choice Specs, Nasty Plot breaker. Insane damage.
- **Flutter Mane**: Fast special sweeper, Calm Mind wincon, Choice Specs.
- **Walking Wake**: Sun sweeper, Hydro Steam + Flamethrower + Draco Meteor terror

### Common Defensive Threats
- **Corviknight**: Physical pivot, Defogger, U-turn momentum. Sets up Bulk Up.
- **Toxapex**: Mixed wall, Regenerator pivot, Toxic/Toxic Spikes, Haze.
- **Blissey/Chansey**: Special walls, cleric, Wish pass, Teleport pivot.
- **Dondozo**: Physical wall, Curse wincon, Unaware ignores boosts.
- **Ting-Lu**: Special wall, Stealth Rock, Whirlwind, Ruination.
- **Ferrothorn**: Mixed wall, Spikes setter, Leech Seed, Iron Barbs.
- **Garganacl**: Mixed wall, Salt Cure chip, Stealth Rock, Curse wincon.
- **Alomomola**: Physical wall, Wish pass, Scald burn, Regenerator pivot.
- **Clodsire**: Special wall, Unaware, Toxic, Stealth Rock, Recover.

### Common Pivots
- **Landorus-Therian**: Ground immunity, Intimidate, Stealth Rock, U-turn
- **Rotom-Wash**: Levitate, Water/Electric coverage, Volt Switch, Will-O-Wisp
- **U-turn Dragapult**: Fast pivot, status spreader, hazard immune
- **Meowscarada**: Fast U-turn, Knock Off, Flower Trick (always crits)
- **Rillaboom**: Grassy Surge (half EQ damage), Grassy Glide priority, U-turn
- **Iron Treads**: Rapid Spin + Stealth Rock + Volt Switch. Ground immune.

## STRATEGIES FOR SPECIFIC POKEMON

### Building Around a Physical Sweeper
1. Identify what walls it (physical walls like Dondozo, Toxapex, Corviknight)
2. Add special attacker that beats those walls
3. Provide hazard support (Stealth Rock chips switch-ins)
4. Consider Magnezone to trap Steel types blocking the sweep
5. Add pivots to bring sweeper in safely

### Building Around a Special Sweeper
1. Identify special walls (Blissey, Clodsire, Ting-Lu)
2. Add physical breaker or trapper for those walls
3. Provide setup opportunities (screens, Memento, U-turn)
4. Hazard support against Sturdy/Focus Sash users
5. Speed control for faster threats

### Building Around a Bulky Setup Sweeper
1. Add cleric support (Heal Bell) to cure status
2. Partner Pokemon that can remove its counters
3. Screens support to ensure setup turn
4. Entry hazard removal (bulky sweepers switch in often)

## WEATHER TEAMS

### Rain Team Core
- Pelipper (Drizzle setter) — mandatory. Slow U-turn for safe switch-ins.
- Swift Swim sweeper: Barraskewda, Floatzel, Kingdra
- Electric type: Zapdos, Thundurus-T (100% accurate Thunder)
- Steel type: Ferrothorn (fire weakness reduced), Scizor
- Ground type: Great Tusk, Gastrodon (cover Electric)
- Rain also enables: Hurricane (100% accurate), Weather Ball (100 BP Water)

### Sun Team Core
- Torkoal (Drought setter) — mandatory. Stealth Rock also.
- Chlorophyll sweeper: Venusaur, Scovillain, Lilligant-Hisui
- Protosynthesis users: Roaring Moon, Flutter Mane, Great Tusk
- Fire types get 1.5x boost: Skeledirge, Volcarona, Iron Moth, Chi-Yu
- Walking Wake is amazing in Sun (Hydro Steam boosted)
- Moonlight/Morning Sun heals 66% in Sun

### Sand Team Core
- Tyranitar or Hippowdon (Sand Stream) — mandatory
- Sand Rush: Excadrill
- Rock/Ground/Steel types get SpD boost
- Garganacl, Skarmory, Ferrothorn fit naturally
- Sand chip damage wears down Focus Sash users

### Snow Team Core
- Alolan Ninetales (Snow Warning + Aurora Veil) — ideal
- Slush Rush: Cetitan, Beartic
- Ice types get +1 Def in Snow
- Aurora Veil = Reflect + Light Screen in one move

## TRICK ROOM (Espacio Raro en español)
- Movimiento de estado tipo Psíquico. Prioridad -7 (siempre se mueve al final).
- Efecto: Invierte el orden de velocidad por 5 turnos. Los Pokémon MÁS LENTOS atacan primero.
- NO es un bioma, NO es una ubicación. Es una alteración del campo de batalla.
- Setters recomendados: Hatterene (Magic Bounce anti-Taunt), Cresselia, Porygon2 (Eviolite), Slowking, Farigiraf, Indeedee-F (Psychic Surge), Gallade
- Abusadores: Ursaluna (Guts + Flame Orb), Torkoal (Eruption), Iron Hands (Booster Energy), Conkeldurr (Guts), Rhyperior, Marowak-Alola (Thick Club), Crawdaunt
- Items clave: Room Service (baja Speed al entrar en Trick Room), Mental Herb (bloquea Taunt), Iron Ball (reduce Speed a la mitad)
- Counter: Hacer Taunt al setter, usar movimientos con prioridad, stallear con Protect, usar tu propio Trick Room para cancelarlo
- Ten al menos 1 Pokémon que funcione fuera de Trick Room por si expira

## TERRAIN TEAMS
- Electric Terrain: Tapu Koko / Pincurchin. Boosts Electric moves, blocks sleep.
- Grassy Terrain: Rillaboom. Halves Earthquake damage, heals 1/16 per turn.
- Psychic Terrain: Indeedee / Tapu Lele. Blocks priority moves, boosts Psychic.
- Misty Terrain: Tapu Fini. Blocks status, halves Dragon damage.
- Key item: Terrain Extender for 8 turns
